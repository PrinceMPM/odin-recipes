Garlic Butter Roasted Mushrooms

Home

Description

Garlic Butter Roasted Mushrooms are a rich, savory side dish that brings out the earthy flavor of mushrooms with the indulgent touch of garlic and melted butter. As they roast in the oven, the mushrooms become tender and juicy, absorbing the garlicky butter and aromatic herbs. The high heat allows them to caramelize slightly on the edges, giving each bite a perfect balance of softness and depth of flavor.

This dish is incredibly versatile—served warm as a side to steak, chicken, or pasta, or even spooned over toast or rice for a simple vegetarian meal. A sprinkle of fresh parsley adds a pop of color and brightness, while a dusting of Parmesan (if used) introduces a nutty, salty finish that complements the umami of the mushrooms beautifully. It’s comfort food with elegance—easy to prepare but rich in taste.

Ingredient

  • 1 pound (450g) whole mushrooms (button or cremini)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or 1 tablespoon fresh)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Clean the mushrooms by gently wiping them with a damp paper towel. Trim the stems if needed.
  3. Place the mushrooms in a large bowl. Add the melted butter, olive oil, minced garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper. Toss until the mushrooms are well coated.
  4. Spread the mushrooms out in a single layer on a baking sheet or in an oven-safe dish.
  5. Roast for 20–25 minutes, stirring once halfway through, until the mushrooms are tender and golden brown.
  6. Optional: Sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese during the last 5 minutes of roasting for extra flavor.
  7. Remove from oven, sprinkle with fresh parsley, and serve hot as a side dish or appetizer.
